Pediatric Blood Needs

Neonatal Transfusion

Newborns with anemia or blood disorders often need urgent transfusions to stabilize their condition and improve survival rates.

Leukemia Treatment

Children undergoing leukemia treatment often require repeated transfusions due to chemotherapy-induced anemia.

Pediatric Surgeries

Children undergoing surgeries often need blood due to small blood volumes and risk of rapid blood loss during operations.

Hemophilia Management

Children with hemophilia need clotting factor transfusions to prevent or control internal bleeding episodes. Early diagnosis and timely treatment are crucial to ensure a healthy and active childhood.

Frequently Asked Questions About Blood Donation

Who can donate blood in India?

To donate blood in India, you must be between 18-65 years old, weigh at least 45kg, and have hemoglobin levels above 12.5g/dL. There should be at least 56 days gap between donations. Certain medical conditions or recent surgeries may defer eligibility.

How often can I donate blood?

Male donors can donate every 3 months, females every 4 months. This recovery time helps maintain safe hemoglobin and health levels.

Is blood donation safe?

Yes, it is safe when done at certified centers using sterilized, single-use equipment. Donors are screened and guided by professionals.
