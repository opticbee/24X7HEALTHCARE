What is Intrauterine Insemination (IUI)?

The IUI Process

IUI is a fertility treatment that places sperm directly in the uterus near ovulation time. It's less invasive and more affordable than IVF, with success rates of 10-20% per cycle for couples with unexplained infertility.

Key Benefits

  • Minimally invasive procedure
  • More affordable than IVF
  • Short recovery time
  • Can be combined with fertility drugs

Ideal Candidates

IUI works best for couples with unexplained infertility, mild male factor infertility, cervical mucus problems, or women with ovulation disorders. It's often the first treatment tried before moving to IVF.

The IUI Treatment Process

1

Initial Consultation

Comprehensive fertility evaluation and personalized treatment plan development with our specialists.

2

Ovarian Stimulation

Fertility medications may be used to stimulate egg production, monitored through ultrasound.

3

Sperm Preparation

Sperm sample is washed and concentrated to select the healthiest sperm for insemination.

4

Insemination Procedure

Quick, painless 5-10 minute procedure where prepared sperm is placed directly in the uterus.

5

Pregnancy Test

Blood test performed about two weeks after IUI to confirm pregnancy.

Preparing for Your IUI Treatment

1 Before Treatment

  • Complete all recommended fertility tests
  • Maintain a healthy diet and exercise routine
  • Avoid alcohol, smoking, and excessive caffeine
  • Take prescribed prenatal vitamins
  • Track your menstrual cycle for several months

2 During Treatment

  • Attend all monitoring appointments
  • Take fertility medications as directed
  • Stay hydrated and maintain light activity
  • Avoid strenuous exercise
  • Practice stress-reduction techniques

3 After Treatment

  • Rest for the remainder of the procedure day
  • Resume normal activities the next day
  • Avoid high-impact exercise for a few days
  • Continue taking prescribed medications
  • Wait for recommended time before taking pregnancy test

Typical IUI Treatment Timeline

1

Day 1-3: Menstrual Cycle Begins

Contact clinic on first day of period to schedule baseline ultrasound and begin treatment cycle.

2

Day 3-7: Fertility Medications

If prescribed, start oral or injectable medications to stimulate follicle development.

3

Day 8-12: Monitoring

Ultrasounds and blood work to track follicle growth and hormone levels.

4

Day 12-14: Trigger Shot

HCG injection to trigger ovulation at optimal time for insemination.

5

Day 14-16: IUI Procedure

Sperm sample is prepared and inserted into uterus via thin catheter (36-40 hours after trigger).

6

Day 28-30: Pregnancy Test

Blood test performed approximately two weeks after IUI to check for pregnancy.

IUI Risks & Side Effects

Procedure Risks

  • Mild cramping or spotting (10-15% of cases)
  • Very low risk of infection (<1%)
  • Rare uterine perforation (extremely uncommon)
  • Minimal discomfort during the procedure

Medication Side Effects

  • Mood swings or irritability
  • Hot flashes or headaches
  • Ovarian hyperstimulation (rare with IUI)
  • Breast tenderness
  • Mild nausea

Pregnancy Considerations

  • Slightly higher chance of multiples (8-10%)
  • No increased risk of birth defects
  • Same miscarriage rates as natural conception
  • Identical pregnancy care after conception

Our medical team carefully monitors all IUI treatments to minimize risks and side effects. We provide detailed counseling before treatment about all potential considerations.

IUI with Donor Sperm

For single women, same-sex couples, or couples with severe male factor infertility, donor sperm IUI offers an effective path to pregnancy.

Donor Selection

We work with accredited sperm banks offering extensive donor profiles including medical history, physical characteristics, education, and personal statements. Some donors provide adult photos or baby pictures.

Legal Considerations

All donors sign away parental rights. We provide legal counseling to ensure proper parental rights establishment, especially for same-sex couples or single mothers by choice.

Success Rates

Donor IUI has slightly higher success rates than partner IUI (15-25% per cycle) due to excellent sperm quality. Most women under 35 conceive within 3 cycles using donor sperm.

IUI Aftercare & Support

Immediately After IUI

  • Rest for 15-30 minutes after procedure
  • Light spotting is normal
  • Mild cramping may occur (use heating pad if needed)
  • Avoid strenuous activity for 24 hours

The Two-Week Wait

  • Continue prescribed medications
  • Avoid excessive exercise
  • Maintain normal daily activities
  • Resist taking early pregnancy tests
  • Practice stress-reduction techniques

IUI Frequently Asked Questions

IUI improves pregnancy chances by placing washed, concentrated sperm directly into the uterus at the time of ovulation. This bypasses potential cervical mucus issues, places sperm closer to the egg, and uses the healthiest sperm from the sample. When combined with ovulation-inducing medications, IUI can significantly increase the number of eggs available for fertilization.
Most women describe IUI as relatively painless, similar to a Pap smear. Some may experience mild cramping during or after the procedure, which typically lasts only a few minutes. The procedure itself takes about 5-10 minutes, and you can return to normal activities immediately afterward with no recovery time needed.
Most fertility specialists recommend 3-6 IUI cycles before considering IVF, as about 80% of pregnancies from IUI occur within the first 3-4 attempts. However, this depends on factors like age, fertility diagnosis, and whether medications are used. Women over 35 may move to IVF sooner. Our specialists will create a personalized plan based on your specific situation.
Standard IUI does not allow for gender selection. Some clinics offer sperm sorting techniques (like MicroSort) combined with IUI that may slightly increase chances of conceiving a particular gender, but these methods are not FDA-approved and have limited success. For reliable gender selection, IVF with preimplantation genetic testing (PGT) is required.
ICI (Intracervical Insemination) places unwashed sperm near the cervix, similar to natural intercourse. IUI places washed sperm directly in the uterus, bypassing the cervix. IVF involves egg retrieval, fertilization in the lab, and embryo transfer. IUI is more effective than ICI but less invasive and expensive than IVF. Success rates increase progressively from ICI to IUI to IVF.
