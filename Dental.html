Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our dental services.

How often should I visit the dentist?

Most patients should visit the dentist every six months for regular checkups and cleanings. However, some patients with specific dental conditions may need more frequent visits. Your dentist will recommend the appropriate schedule for your needs.

What should I do in case of a dental emergency?

In case of a dental emergency, call our emergency line immediately. For a knocked-out tooth, keep it moist (preferably in milk) and come to our office within 30 minutes if possible. For severe pain or bleeding, don't wait – contact us right away.

Do you accept dental insurance?

Yes, we accept most major dental insurance plans. Our administrative team will help verify your coverage and maximize your benefits. We also offer payment plans for those without insurance or for procedures not fully covered.

At what age should my child first visit the dentist?

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends that children visit the dentist by their first birthday or when their first tooth erupts. Early visits help establish good oral health habits and make dental visits a positive experience.

How can I improve my smile?

We offer numerous cosmetic dentistry options including teeth whitening, veneers, bonding, and Invisalign. During a consultation, our dentist will assess your smile and discuss the best options to achieve your desired results.
